# Concept Reel Shotlist

## Purpose
Create an execution-ready surrogate reel that explains how the current browser ident could evolve into a truthful spatial prototype.

## Disclosure Card
- Duration: `2s`
- Content: `Concept evaluation. Browser runtime exists. XR behavior shown only as planned extension.`
- Purpose: establish honesty before atmosphere.

## Shot 1: Logo Under Pressure
- Duration: `4s`
- Visual: current centered `speedrun` mark emerges over a dimmed racing backplate with restrained scanlines and slow wake buildup.
- Motion behavior: no burst yet; only idle pulse and minor lateral drift.
- Teaching beat: establish the project's real current identity surface.

## Shot 2: Decorative Telemetry Called Out
- Duration: `4s`
- Visual: the current node web and pulses appear, then specific nodes are labeled `decorative`, `not data-bound`, and `ambient`.
- Motion behavior: freeze and isolate layers one by one.
- Teaching beat: show the gap between impressive motion and actual system meaning.

## Shot 3: Signal Wake Reframe
- Duration: `5s`
- Visual: the web reorganizes into three or four named lane markers suspended in depth, with wake trails now attached to those lanes instead of floating freely.
- Motion behavior: wake trails converge toward one active lane while other lanes dim.
- Teaching beat: convert motion from mood into structure.

## Shot 4: Deliberate Selection
- Duration: `5s`
- Visual: a gaze ring settles on a lane marker and a low-fatigue confirm action triggers the next state.
- Motion behavior: dwell ring completes; speed lines contract inward instead of simply accelerating outward.
- Teaching beat: introduce intentional interaction instead of agitation-only input.

## Shot 5: Consequence View
- Duration: `5s`
- Visual: the selected lane opens into a forward corridor of pulses, status markers, and a relocated logo acting as a corner state badge.
- Motion behavior: one failure pulse and one success pulse travel at distinct speeds.
- Teaching beat: show that the system can encode outcome, not just intensity.

## Shot 6: Return to Brand Frame
- Duration: `3s`
- Visual: the environment collapses back into the existing hero ident composition.
- Motion behavior: burst effect returns, but now it reads as a chapter return rather than pure spectacle.
- Teaching beat: preserve the brand asset while proving the bridge to a more explanatory prototype.

## Notes
- Keep the reel under `30s`.
- Use callouts or title cards whenever a visual changes semantic meaning.
- Do not imply that lane naming, selection logic, or XR depth is already implemented.
